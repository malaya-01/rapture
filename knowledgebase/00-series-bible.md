# Series Bible — Rapture

## Elevator pitch

When the sky fractures and the world ends in a single afternoon, an ordinary omega named Cassian Reed survives among scattered strangers who will become family, founders, and rivals — while the slow, stubborn bond between him and alpha Adrian Hale grows across centuries of catastrophe, rebuilding, and choice.

## Target length

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Total chapters** | **1200** |
| **Audience** | **Adult (18+)** — `23-content-maturity-rating.md` |
| Preferred range | 1100–1300 |
| Words per chapter | 1,800–2,400 |
| Estimated series | ~2.4M words |

Compress from the original 1800-chapter design by merging volume beats — **never** pad chapters for count.

## Pacing contract (*Walking Dead* model)

| Milestone | Chapter target |
|-----------|----------------|
| Rapture begins | **Chapter 1** |
| Civilization collapsing | **Chapters 2–4** |
| Monsters on page | **Chapters 4–5** |
| Main cast paths cross | **Before Chapter 20** |
| First dungeon sighting | **~Chapter 30** |
| Adrian + Cassian meet | **~Chapter 81** (friendship only) |

Apocalypse is not backstory. It is the opening movement.

## Romance contract (alpha / omega)

- **Cassian Reed** — omega; spatial affinity; founder; not chosen one
- **Adrian Hale** — alpha; protector; co-founder
- End state: together as partners (late saga)
- **Not** the only focus — journey, civilization, cast, and mysteries share equal weight
- No romance before genuine friendship (meet ~Ch 81; attraction much later)
- Selene/Adrian relationship must read **real** until her tragic arc

## Three Ages (macro structure)

1. **Age 1 — Survival** (Ch ~1–350): Can humanity survive?
2. **Age 2 — Rebuilding** (Ch ~351–850): Can humanity recover?
3. **Age 3 — Unification** (Ch ~851–1200): What world should exist?

## Antagonist rule

**No single Dark Lord.** Threats layer:

- Tragic (Selene)
- Human (Viktor, Adrian Voss warlord, Mother Seraphine)
- Monster Lords (Skarax, Velkarion, Brood Mother)
- Dungeon Sovereigns (Pale King, Clockwork Empress)
- Political rivals
- Ancient beings (Azrael — incomplete)
- Final crisis (emerges from worldbuilding — Seventh Gate / cycles)

## Non-negotiables

- Magic has **limits**; no omnipotent Cassian
- Contracts are **voluntary** — no slavery / monster collection
- Antagonists have goals, philosophy, strengths, weaknesses, relationships
- Final conflict is **not** good vs evil — competing visions of the future
- Write as **literary novel prose** — never screenplay/scene labels in published chapters
- **Adult (18+) honesty** — do not soften violence, hazard, or brutal consequences; romance may include affection and kissing; no explicit sex on page (`23-content-maturity-rating.md`)

## Content maturity

See **`23-content-maturity-rating.md`**. Short form: this series is for adults. Combat and survival are **visceral**. Death and injury have weight. Monster and war scenes are not bloodless. Romantic payoff includes **tenderness and kissing** — not pornographic detail.

## Sellability

- Strong hook in Ch 1 (sky breaks)
- Ensemble cast with rotating POV
- Clear progression: survival → settlement → nation → world
- Romance payoff for long-term readers without hijacking plot
- Franchise bible supports 1200 chapters without directionless filler
