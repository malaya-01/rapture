### Appearance

Atlas inherits Rowan's breadth in miniature — not yet massive, but already built like a boy who climbs fences and carries firewood without being asked. Dark brown hair, usually escaping whatever braid Elena manages in the morning. Hazel eyes like his father's, but softer when he looks at wounded animals or younger children he has appointed himself protector of.

Pre-Rapture he wore hand-me-down jackets and sneakers too large, cuffs turned up. Post-Rapture growth tracks Sanctuary's growth: scavenged leathers → proper ranger kit → Guard officer gear that still looks slightly too adult on his shoulders until it doesn't. A scar along one forearm from an early Ashwolf territory patrol he refuses to show off.

By the bond era (~Ch 845), he stands beside **Ash** — dire ashwolf companion, black fur with ember markings, amber eyes — and looks like someone who learned leadership from watching Rowan hold a line, not from wanting a title.

### Personality

Determined, brave, impatient with politics. Atlas believes action should precede committee. He loves his father and resents being introduced as "Rowan's son" in the same breath — not from shame, but from hunger to earn a name that fits.

He is kind in the Rowan mold: rough tenderness, practical help, jokes that land badly when he is nervous. Sibling rivalry with Leon is real but never cruel; Atlas protects Leon's books the way Leon protects Atlas's secrets.

### Pre-Rapture life

Raised on Ashford's outskirts in the Hale family home — lawn, dinner table, firefighter stories at holidays. Atlas was the child who asked "what if" during drills and got answered with patience. On Rapture day he was young enough to trust adults completely and old enough to remember the sky wrong forever.

He grew up inside Sanctuary's walls: refugee camps becoming streets, streets becoming districts. He learned to walk patrol routes before he learned algebra.

### Magic and affinity

**Affinity:** None primary (physical excellence, tactical instinct, beast contract synergy).

**Companion:** **Ash** — dire ashwolf line; brave, stubborn, protective. Evolution path: Ash Cub → Ashwolf → Dire Ashwolf → Ember Fang → Ash King. Voluntary beast contract ~Ch 845. See `15-companions-beast-contracts.md`.

**Artifact (late saga, optional):** Inherits Guard traditions; not a mythic weapon wielder — leadership is his craft.

### Appearance

Leon is slighter than Atlas — beta build, narrow shoulders, posture bent toward books and tablets rather than horizons. Dark hair like the Hale men; eyes that catch mana light a half-second before others see rifts, pale gold rim when vision activates. Pre-Rapture he wore glasses; post-Rapture Marcus crafts rune lenses that do not fog during winter patrols Leon insists on joining anyway.

Ink-stained fingers by adolescence. A satchel always heavy with borrowed archives. He dresses in scholar layers — too many pockets, scarf his mother knit, boots that are never quite rugged enough for where Nora's maps lead him.

Late saga: looks young beside the weight of what he knows — cycle fragments, Gate readings, truths leaders prefer softened.

### Personality

Curious, quiet, observant. Leon speaks when precision matters; silence is not fear but sorting. He keeps truth when councils prefer comfort — not from cruelty, but because he watched people die from wrong information once and cannot unwatch it.

Adrian mentors his ethical framework: protect without dominating, know without wielding knowledge as a blade. Leon's flaw is isolation — he retreats into archives when grief demands company.

### Pre-Rapture life

Younger Hale son; learned to read early and hid in closets with library books when adults argued about money. Rapture day memory: his mother's hands on his face, counting breaths; his father's back blocking the doorway.

Sanctuary childhood: Marcus as unofficial uncle; Academy founded when Leon is old enough to resent being too young for enrollment, then young enough to be its brightest embarrassment.

### Magic and affinity

**Affinity:** Mana vision (rare perceptual gift — not combat magic).

**Ability:** Perceives mana density, corruption veins, hidden runes, fracture geometry. Does not make him a fighter; makes him indispensable. Dragons notice when he looks at them too long.

**Development:** Flickers childhood → trained discipline → partial **cycle truth** keeper Vol 18–20 → elder scholar whose notebooks outlive him.

See `10-magic-and-mana-system.md` — mana vision listed alongside advanced mages and dragons.

### Appearance

Iris has a warm face that people trust before she speaks — soft jaw, laugh lines she earned young, brown eyes that remember names. Hair chestnut, usually tied back with whatever strip of cloth is clean. Practical skirts and boots because she kneels beside children more than she sits in chairs.

She smells like bread when Sanctuary has ovens again, like chalk when she runs impromptu school in tents, like rain when she has been carrying laundry across courtyards. No armor; a reinforced vest after the second raid season. Ink on her fingers from schedules, not runes.

### Personality

Kind, patient, immovable in matters of children. Iris is the community's memory of normal — birthdays observed when possible, stories at dusk, the rule that no one eats alone if she can help it.

Her strength is logistics of care: who needs shoes, who flinches at loud sounds, which teenager is pretending to be fine. Her flaw is overextension — she will absorb everyone's grief until Marcus or Selene (early) or Eva (later) forces her to sleep.

### Pre-Rapture life

**Elementary-school aide and part-time community volunteer** near Ashford's university district — the job that paid between Marcus's grants and Lily's needs. She married Marcus for his gentleness, not his fame (he had none). Their home was cluttered with books, Lily's drawings, and Iris's lists.

Rapture day: evacuation in the family car; Marcus driving, Iris keeping Lily calm, world ending in the rearview. She became organizer before anyone gave her a title because children were standing in mud and adults were shouting.

### Magic and affinity

**Affinity:** None. Strength is social architecture — schedules, morale, integration, civilian networks.

Works closely with **Selene Arkwright** early saga on refugee intake; fracture late saga when Selene's methods diverge.

Strategic, articulate, morally serious. Isabella chose Sanctuary when she could have chosen empire. She believes law is how survivors avoid becoming what they fled.
