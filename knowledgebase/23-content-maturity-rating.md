# Content Maturity Rating — Adult (18+)

**Audience:** Adult readers. This is a literary survival epic, not YA or middle-grade fiction.

All writers, editors, and AI agents must treat Rapture as **mature fiction**. Do not sanitize, soften, or skip difficult material when the story demands it. Do not default to “safe” or “family-friendly” tone.

---

## Rating summary

| Area | Policy |
|------|--------|
| **Overall** | 18+ — consequences, violence, and intimacy are written honestly |
| **Sex / explicit erotica** | **Not on page.** No pornographic detail, no step-by-step sexual acts |
| **Romance & affection** | **On page** when earned — hand-holding, embraces, kisses, longing, jealousy, grief over love, domestic intimacy, implied sex (fade or cut) |
| **Violence & combat** | **Full weight** — blood, gore, broken bones, exhaustion, fear, death, aftermath; not anime-clean |
| **Horror & hazard** | **Full weight** — starvation, infection, amputation, psychological break, cruelty, monster feeding, environmental death |
| **Language** | Strong language when character and moment warrant it — not every paragraph |
| **Illustrations** | Mature premium novel art; combat may show blood and injury; romance tasteful, never explicit |

---

## What to write without holding back

- **Brutal fights:** messy, costly, frightening; injuries linger; armor fails; people die badly
- **Monster encounters:** predation, dismemberment, swarm consumption, infection — as ecology, not cartoon violence
- **Survival hardship:** rationing, hypothermia, untreated wounds, moral compromise under pressure
- **Political cruelty:** executions, sieges, refugee suffering, occupation — shown through POV consequence
- **Trauma & grief:** panic attacks, survivor guilt, numbness, rage — not resolved in one scene
- **Antagonist acts:** Viktor’s order, cult manipulation, dungeon horrors — depicted, not summarized off-screen

**Rule:** If a scene would lose honesty by cutting away, **stay in the POV** and show what they perceive — sensory, emotional, physical cost.

## Romance & intimacy (prose)

Rapture is a **slow-burn** romance saga. Physical love is **earned late** — but when it arrives, do not write as if the book is PG-13.

### Allowed on page

- Flirting, tension, jealousy, heartbreak
- Touch, hand-holding, embraces, foreheads together
- **Kissing** — brief or sustained, tender or desperate, as the moment requires
- Waking beside someone, shared bed, undressing to a certain acceptable but the excessive is implied
- Aftermath of intimacy — emotional, not mechanical
- Aria/Cassian and Adrian/Cassian arcs: mature partnership, not euphemism-only

### Not allowed on page

- Explicit pornographic description of sex acts
- Fetish content written for titillation detached from character
- Reducing omega/alpha dynamics to sexual stereotypes or breeding rhetoric

### Fade / cut technique

For intercourse: cut to before or after, or a short implied beat (“later, in the dark…”). **Affection before and after is not fade territory** — kisses and closeness stay visible.

---

## Violence (prose)

- **Do not** replace gore with “they clashed blades and someone fell”
- **Do** use POV-limited detail: what Cassian smells, hears, cannot unsee
- Healing (Elena) does not erase that violence happened — scars, trauma, triage scenes matter
- Death of named characters: weight and consequence; no respawn mentality

Reference tone: literary grimdark survival (*The Walking Dead*, *The Road*, mature epic fantasy) — not shonen, not cozy fantasy.

---

## What NOT to do

- Skip blood or injury “because it’s too much”
- Replace kisses with endless subtext once the relationship is established (late saga)
- Write combat as bloodless choreography
- Sanitize Rapture Day, dungeon runs, or war chapters into summary
- Add explicit sex scenes to “mature” the book — maturity is **honesty**, not erotica
- Shock for shock’s sake without POV or plot purpose

---

## Agent & chapter checklist

Before publishing a chapter, confirm:

1. Did any fight, injury, or death get softened against bible intent?
2. Did any earned romantic beat get skipped from caution?
3. Is any sexual content explicit (if yes, revise to implied/fade)?
4. Does hazard/survival read **real** to the POV character?
