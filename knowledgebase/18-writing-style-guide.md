# Writing Style Guide

## Format: NOVEL PROSE ONLY

Published chapters in `content/chapters/` are **literary fiction**.

### Forbidden in chapter text

- `INT.` / `EXT.` scene headings  
- `POV:` labels in body text  
- Bullet beat lists or `MAJOR EVENTS:` blocks  
- Screenplay dialogue formatting (`CHARACTER NAME` centered lines)  
- Game/system notifications (`[Quest Complete]`, level-ups)

### Required

- **Close third person, past tense** (default POV per chapter)  
- Paragraphs of flowing prose — dialogue integrated naturally  
- Scene breaks: `* * *` only when time/place jumps significantly  
- Multi-POV vignette labels: `## I — Character Name` on its own line (compiled to styled subheads)
- Chapter metadata in **YAML frontmatter only** (not in body)

## Frontmatter template

```yaml
---
chapter: 1
title: "The Last Ordinary Morning"
pov: Cassian Reed
volume: 1
arc: "The Last Ordinary Days"
in_world_day: 0
characters: [cassian-reed]
locations: [ashford]
status: draft
---
```

## Sensory palette

Ozone at rifts · wet ash · iodine · diesel · rain on concrete · copper (mana) · cold silver light from fractures

## Pacing

Match *Walking Dead* urgency in Vol 1 — no 50-page normal-life prologue. Quiet chapters earn their slowness after Ch 10.

## Romance prose

Subtext dominates **early** saga. Once relationships are established (late saga), **do not shy away** from affection, embraces, or **kissing on page**.

- **Allowed:** longing, jealousy, hand-holding, embraces, kisses, shared bed implied, emotional aftermath of intimacy  
- **Not on page:** explicit pornographic description of sex acts — use fade or implication  
- Never reduce characters to alpha/omega stereotypes  

Full policy: `23-content-maturity-rating.md`

## Combat prose

**Adult (18+).** Messy, frightening, costly — **not** anime choreography or bloodless summary.

- Show injury, blood, exhaustion, fear, and death through POV-limited sensory detail  
- Named deaths carry weight; triage and healing scenes acknowledge damage  
- Do not cut away from brutality to protect the reader when the scene demands honesty  

## Hazard & horror prose

Starvation, infection, amputation, psychological break, predator feeding, and environmental death are **in bounds** when plot-relevant. Do not summarize off-screen what the POV would witness.

## AI phrase watchlist

Avoid overuse: *delve, tapestry, testament, shiver down spine, orbs (for eyes), palpable tension*

## Quality bar

Every chapter should read like a **publishable novel excerpt** — the kind that sells on voice and character, not outline summary.
